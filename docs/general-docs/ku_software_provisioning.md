# KU Edwards Campus Software Provisioning Guide

This document outlines the free and discounted software available to students, faculty, and staff at the University of Kansas Edwards Campus, as well as instructions for accessing and downloading these applications.

## Microsoft Office 365 (Free for Students, Faculty, and Staff)
All current KU students, faculty, and staff have free access to the full Microsoft Office 365 suite, which includes Word, Excel, PowerPoint, OneNote, and 5TB of OneDrive cloud storage.

**How to Download and Install:**
You can install Microsoft 365 on up to 5 personal devices (Windows, Mac, iOS, or Android).
1. Go to the Microsoft 365 login page (office.com).
2. Log in using your KU email address (KU_Online_ID@ku.edu) and your KU password.
3. Once logged in, click the "Install Office" or "Install Apps" button near the top right of the dashboard.
4. Select "Microsoft 365 apps" to begin the download.
5. Follow the standard installation prompts on your device.

## Adobe Creative Cloud
Adobe applications (such as Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, and Premiere Pro) are available to the KU community, though access varies by role:
* **Students:** Students can access the full Adobe Creative Cloud suite for free on any campus computer lab workstation at the Edwards Campus. 
* **Faculty and Staff:** Faculty and staff can download Adobe Creative Cloud applications for home use and on KU-owned workstations. 

## KU Software Web Store
The KU Software Web Store is the central hub for accessing additional specialized software required for courses and research. 
* **Available Software:** Depending on your coursework and role, you can find free or heavily discounted licenses for software such as SPSS, SAS, MATLAB, JMP, and EndNote.
* **How to Access:** Visit the KU IT website and navigate to the Software Web Store. Log in with your KU Online ID to view the specific catalog available to you.

## KU Virtual Lab (Windows Virtual Desktop)
If you need to use specialized software but cannot install it on your personal computer (e.g., you are using a Chromebook or a low-spec laptop), you can use the KU Virtual Lab.
* The Virtual Lab allows you to remotely log into a KU campus desktop environment directly from your web browser.
* You will have access to the standard suite of software installed on physical Edwards Campus lab computers.
* *Note:* Ensure you save all your work to your KU OneDrive while using the Virtual Lab, as local files are wiped when your virtual session ends.
