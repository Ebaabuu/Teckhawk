# KU Duo Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Guide

This document outlines the policies and procedures for using Duo Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) at the University of Kansas and the Edwards Campus. Duo MFA is required for all KU faculty, staff, and students to access systems protected by Single Sign-On (SSO), such as Canvas, Enroll & Pay, and HR/Pay.

## Authentication Methods
KU IT highly recommends using the Duo Mobile app on a smartphone as your primary authentication method.

* **Duo Push (Recommended):** Sends a notification to your smartphone. You simply tap "Approve" to log in.
* **Duo Mobile Passcode:** The Duo app generates a 6-digit passcode. This method works offline, even if you do not have cell service or a Wi-Fi connection.
* **Text Message / Phone Call:** You can elect to receive a passcode via SMS text or receive an automated phone call to verify your identity.
* **Hardware Tokens:** For users who cannot use a smartphone, KU IT provides hardware display tokens. Tokens generate a passcode when a button is pressed. Accounts are prohibited from having more than one token assigned at a time. If lost, they are replaced at no charge. 

## "Remember Me" Feature
When logging into a KU service, you can select the "Remember me for 30 days" option. This allows you to bypass the Duo prompt for 30 days, provided you are using the exact same device and the exact same web browser. 
* **Note:** When connecting to the KU VPN, you must authenticate with Duo every time. The "Remember Me" feature does not apply to VPN connections.

## Device Management & New Phones
If you get a new phone, you must transfer your Duo account to maintain access to KU systems. It is highly recommended to enroll a secondary device (such as a tablet or landline) to your account to prevent being locked out.

**How to add a new device or reactivate a new phone:**
1. Log in to a KU SSO system to trigger the Duo prompt.
2. Do not send a push. Instead, click "Other Options" (or "My Settings & Devices").
3. Click "Manage devices" at the bottom of the prompt.
4. Authenticate using your existing/old device or a secondary enrolled device.
5. Select "Add a device" and follow the on-screen prompts to register your new phone and scan the new QR code.

**Locked out?** If you no longer have access to your old phone and do not have a secondary device enrolled, you cannot bypass the Duo prompt yourself. You must contact the IT Help Desk for an identity verification bypass.

## International Travel Restrictions
Like many platforms licensed by KU, Duo complies with U.S. regulations related to embargoed countries and regions (OFAC regulations). 
* KU users attempting to authenticate from an IP address originating in an OFAC-regulated country or region will be blocked from logging in. 
* If you are traveling internationally to non-restricted countries and will not have cell service or Wi-Fi, rely on the "Passcode" generation feature within the Duo Mobile App.
