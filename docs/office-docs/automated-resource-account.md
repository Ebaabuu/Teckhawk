# Automated Resource Account - Information and Use

## Automated Booking Resource Accounts - FAQ

### What is an Automated Booking Resource Account?

An Automated Booking Resource account can provide a quick response (accept or decline) to requests for scheduling conference rooms and equipment. Not all conference rooms and equipment in Outlook are set up for automated booking.

### Can I log in directly to a Resource Account?

No. The Moderator manages the account through their own Outlook Calendar view or Calendar groups. To learn more, view the Managing Rooms guide.

### How do I request a new Automated Booking Account?

If you are the person responsible for managing a room or equipment resource, please visit the request link in the KU IT Service Portal. From there, you can request a resource account for Rooms or Equipment.

### How do I change an Automated Resource?

Contact the KU IT Customer Service Center at 785-864-8080 or itcsc@ku.edu.

### Who is authorized to send meeting requests to schedule rooms?

Any KU Outlook user is permitted to send a meeting request to schedule a resource displayed in Outlook. Depending on room availability and how the room permissions are configured, the request may be automatically accepted, automatically denied, or submitted to a Moderator.

### How do I schedule a meeting that is configured as an Automated Booking Account?

See instructions in the "Scheduling a Meeting" section below.

### What if my meeting request is Declined by the room?

Review the information provided in the scheduling response. Use of the room or equipment may be restricted to a particular department or group, or the resource may have already been booked.

### Does the "Room Finder" tool work for this kind of account?

Yes, the Outlook Room Finder feature works the same as previously.

### My attendees have not received the meeting request. Why?

Not all rooms are configured for automated booking. Rooms that are NOT configured for automatic booking must be manually reviewed by the Moderator before the request goes to attendees.

### What about equipment such as vehicles or projectors? Does the process change?

No. Automated resource accounts can also be created for equipment. You will select equipment the same way you add an individual. If you have problems finding equipment, search the Global Address List.

## For Resource Account Users

To schedule a meeting using an Automated Booking Resource, see the instructions below. If you schedule a meeting that includes a room and do not receive an immediate response, that room may not be converted yet. If a room you use has not been converted, please contact your departmental technical support staff to request that transition.

Automated Resource Accounts allow you to schedule rooms when setting up meetings in Outlook. This includes the functionality to see when a particular room is booked and includes the ability to moderate requests for rooms if desired.

## For Resource Account Moderators

There are 3 different types of room management:

- **Moderated:** Requests to reserve the room must be approved or denied by the designated Moderator.
- **Self-service group(s):** Requests are automatically accepted for designated groups. Requests from people not in those groups can be manually reviewed or automatically denied.
- **Self-service for all:** Requests from any user are automatically approved.

To make changes to an Automatic Resource, contact the KU IT Customer Service Center.

## Scheduling a Meeting Using an Automated Resource Booking Account

1. Open **Outlook**.
2. Select **New Items** and choose **New Meeting**.
3. Switch to **Scheduling Assistant** view within the meeting request.
4. Add your meeting **Attendees** as usual.
   - Note: If you need to include equipment, select the equipment resources as attendees the same way you add an individual. Search the Global Address List if you have trouble finding equipment.
5. Choose **Add Rooms** to select your meeting room.
6. Select the room(s) you need for your appointment in the room finder window and click **OK**.
7. The room will display in the attendees window with the room icon, indicating it is a resource account.
8. Complete your meeting request and click **Send**.
9. When the room is booked successfully, you will receive a meeting acceptance or decline based on the availability and configuration of the room.
