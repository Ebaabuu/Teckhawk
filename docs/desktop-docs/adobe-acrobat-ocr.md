# Adobe Acrobat - Performing Optical Character Recognition (OCR)

## Summary

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is a feature within Adobe Acrobat that recognizes non-interactable/non-searchable text within an image and converts it into interactive and searchable text within a PDF document.

## Prerequisites

- **Adobe Acrobat License:** Provided to KU faculty and staff.
- **OneDrive:** Installed on your computer (for cloud-integrated workflows).
- **Permissions:** Proper write permissions for SharePoint or local file locations.

## How to Perform OCR on Files

### Initial Setup

1. Open **Adobe Acrobat** on your device.
2. From the Home screen, click on **See all tools**.
3. Scroll down to find **Scan & OCR** and click **Open**.

### For a Single File

1. Click **Select a file** and choose your document.
2. Click **Start**.
3. Under the **Recognize Text** menu on the left, click **In this file**.
4. Click the **Recognize Text** button.
5. Once complete, click the **Save** icon in the top-right to save your searchable PDF.

### For Multiple Files (Batch Processing)

1. Click **Or recognize text in multiple files**.
2. Select all files you wish to convert and click **OK**.
3. In the **Output Options** window, configure the following:
   - **Target Folder:** Select "The Same Folder Selected at Start" or a specific "Folder on My Computer."
   - **File Naming:** Select "Add to original file names" to avoid overwriting.
   - **Insert After:** Type `-text` to differentiate the new file (e.g., `file-text.pdf`).
4. In **General Settings**, set your language and choose **Searchable Image (Exact)** for the best quality.
5. Click **OK** to begin processing.

> **Note:** Batch processing large or numerous files may take several minutes.
