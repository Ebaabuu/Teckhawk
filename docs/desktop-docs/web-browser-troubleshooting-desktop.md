# Web Browser - Troubleshooting Tips for Desktops and Laptops

Many KU web services use pop-up windows to display information. Depending on the service or the browser, you may need to allow pop-ups. Common KU services that use pop-ups include Enroll & Pay, HR/Pay, myKU, MyTalent, and the Student Housing app.

## 1. Allow Pop-up Windows

To ensure KU web services function correctly, you should allow pop-ups for the ku.edu domain.

**General Rule:** Most browsers will display an icon in the address bar when a pop-up is blocked. Click this icon to select "Always allow pop-ups from ku.edu".

## 2. Clear Cache & Cookies

Cached files can cause errors on websites, including login issues and display problems. Clearing your cache and cookies is often the first step in resolving these issues.

**Shortcut:** For all major browsers, press **Ctrl + Shift + Del** (or **Cmd + Shift + Delete** on macOS) to open the clear browsing data menu.

**Recommendation:** After clearing your cache and cookies, it is highly recommended to close and reopen your browser.

## 3. Enable Third-Party Cookies

Systems such as Canvas and Microsoft Integration with OneDrive require you to allow third-party cookies to function properly.

**Action:** Check your browser's Privacy and Security settings to ensure third-party cookies are not blocked for KU-related sites.

## 4. Open a Private Browsing Window

Private (Incognito) windows are useful for circumventing logged-in accounts without logging out or preventing a website from accessing your existing browser cache.

**Common Method:** Right-click your browser icon and select:
- **Chrome:** New Incognito Window
- **Firefox/Safari:** New Private Window
- **Edge:** New InPrivate Window

## 5. Trusted Sites & Extensions

- **Trusted Sites:** Adding *.ku.edu to your browser's "Trusted Sites" list can resolve many security-related display issues.
- **Browser Extensions:** If a site is still not working, try turning off browser extensions (like ad blockers), as they can sometimes interfere with KU web services.

## 6. Viewing Saved Credentials

If you can access an account with a saved credential but cannot log in by typing your password, you can verify your password against the saved data on your device.

**Note:** A saved credential is not guaranteed to be the current or correct one.
