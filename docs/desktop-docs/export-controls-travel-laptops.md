# Export Controls - Travel Laptops (Employee Only)

## Overview

This guide assists KU employees in understanding and using university-provided laptops for international travel. Due to host country regulations, access to standard online resources may be restricted. Users should use the **Virtual Desktop (AVD)** environment to maintain secure access to KU resources.

## FAQ

- **Sign-in Format:** Use your `onlineID@home.ku.edu` to log into the device.
- **Available Hardware:** Currently, only **Windows-based** devices are available for travel loaners.
- **Device Return:** Laptops must be returned to the IT Customer Service Center within **5 business days** of your return date.
- **Remote Office Access:** Direct remote connections to your office computer are prohibited; all work should be done via the AVD environment.
- **Personal Software/VPNs:** Web-based services (Dropbox, Spotify, etc.) are allowed, but installing personal software or VPNs is strictly prohibited.
- **Data Storage:** Do **not** store sensitive data locally on the laptop or use external media like thumb drives. All data should be accessed and saved via the Virtual Desktop (AVD).
- **Password Security:** Laptops are wiped upon return. It is strongly recommended to change your password upon returning from international travel.

## Travel Laptop Initial Setup

**Important:** Do **not** attempt to set up the laptop on a KU campus network (except eduRoam), as it will fail. Complete this setup on a trusted home network before your departure.

1. **Power On:** Turn on the device and connect to your local Wi-Fi.
2. **Login:** Use your credentials in the `onlineID@home.ku.edu` format.
3. **Application Verification:** Ensure the **Microsoft Windows App** (formerly Remote Desktop) is installed and functional.

## Accessing International Virtual Desktop (AVD)

The AVD environment simulates a secure on-campus computer.

1. **Launch App:** Open the **Windows App** (or "Remote Desktop" on older configurations).
2. **Sign In:** Use your `onlineID@home.ku.edu` email and authenticate via KU's Single Sign-On (SSO).
3. **Connect:** Select the **"AVD-INTERNATIONAL"** or **"International AVD Desktop"** icon and click **Connect**.
4. **Permissions:** When prompted, allow access to your **camera and microphone** to ensure Zoom and Teams function correctly.
5. **Saving Work:** Always save files to your **KU OneDrive** folder. Files saved locally to the virtual desktop are deleted when the session ends.

## Duo and General Support

### Duo Multi-Factor Authentication

- **Travel Tip:** Do not change your SIM card if you rely on Duo Push notifications. If you lack cell service/Wi-Fi, open the Duo app to generate a **6-digit passcode** for offline authentication.
- **Restrictions:** Duo authentication is blocked in OFAC-regulated countries (e.g., Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria).
